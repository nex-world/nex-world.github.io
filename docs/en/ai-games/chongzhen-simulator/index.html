When AI Becomes Your Court and Courtiers — “Historical Simulator: Chongzhen”

A game where you write imperial edicts in natural language and the AI simulates the state of the world. No preset options, no scripted scenarios — every word you write could rewrite history.

👉 Steam Store Page 

This Is Not a Traditional Historical Simulation Game

Imagine yourself sitting on the dragon throne. Shaanxi Province is suffering a drought, the Later Jin cavalry is eyeing you from Liaodong, the imperial treasury lacks silver to pay next month’s army wages, and every courtier in the hall has their own agenda — what do you do?

In traditional historical strategy games, you can only pick the least terrible option from a handful of preset choices. But in “Historical Simulator: Chongzhen,” whatever you write, the AI executes.

You could write: “Open the granaries, relieve the famine-stricken, and order local governments to organize water conservancy projects.” You could also write: “I, the Emperor, shall seek immortality. Summon all alchemists from across the realm to the palace.” Or even: “Research the steam engine, form a musket corps, and build a steel industry.”

The AI takes every single word you write seriously.

💡

Game Positioning: China’s first truly AI-native commercial game — the large language model is not a decorative gimmick but the core engine driving the entire game world.

Core Gameplay: Decree → Simulation → Observe Results

The game progresses in turn-based fashion. Each turn presents you with a real dynastic crisis. The basic loop consists of three steps:

1. Assess the Situation 📊

Open the multi-dimensional map view — standard, disaster, public sentiment, unrest, city defense, taxation… understand what is happening in your empire right now. Shaanxi has been in drought for three months, military pressure is mounting in Liaodong, and the Jiangnan gentry are hoarding goods. First grasp the situation, then make your decision.

2. Court Assembly 🗣️

Summon your ministers to the court. Cao Huachun, Wei Zhongxian, Yuan Chonghuan… each has an AI-driven independent personality and agenda. The eunuch clique advises you to suppress the Donglin faction; the Donglin faction urges you to purge the eunuch clique — you cannot fully trust anyone, because each has their own calculations. Their advice is generated in real time, making every court assembly different.

3. Issue an Edict ✍️

This is the game’s most central and unique feature — you write the imperial edict in natural language.

No A/B options, no dialogue trees, no multiple-choice questions. Before you is a blank input field. Whatever you write, the AI executes within the game.

Example One: “Decree to the Ministry of Revenue: Immediately allocate one hundred thousand taels of silver to purchase grain for transport to Shaanxi. Local garrisons shall escort the shipment along the way. Anyone who dares to intercept or embezzle shall be beheaded.” → AI simulation: The Minister of Revenue receives the decree, the silver is allocated, and grain shipments begin moving to Shaanxi. But the road from Jiangnan to Shaanxi is long — whether the checkpoints cooperate, whether the military actually provides support, and how the grain is distributed at the destination are all details the AI simulates based on the current game state.

Example Two: “I, the Emperor, shall establish an Academy of Sciences. Summon all skilled artisans and scholars from across the realm to research new firearms and navigation technologies.” → AI simulation: The academy is established, expenditures increase, and conservative court officials begin submitting memorials opposing “frivolous ingenuity.” How will you handle these cascading consequences?

How Does the AI Drive This World?

Many people ask: isn’t this just a chat game wrapped in an AI shell?

The answer is no. The AI system of “Historical Simulator: Chongzhen” uses a three-tier architecture:

  1. State Constraint Layer — Ensures the AI’s simulations always align with the game’s current historical context and data state (e.g., if the treasury is empty, “declare a general amnesty and exempt taxes for three years” would be judged infeasible)
  2. Intent Judgment Layer — Identifies the player’s true intent behind a command and determines whether it fits the game’s tone (writing “seek immortality” is allowed, but triggers corresponding cost mechanisms; writing “build an atomic bomb” won’t succeed directly — you need to advance the tech tree step by step)
  3. State Persistence Layer — Converts simulation results into structured data and writes them to the database — the world state has truly changed

In other words, when you order “dismantle the courier stations,” the AI not only simulates the money saved but also simulates where the unemployed couriers end up — and among them is a man named Li Zicheng.

That’s the butterfly effect.

  • One player spent 70 hours climbing the tech tree from 1627 — the Industrial Revolution → electricity → uranium enrichment → building an atomic bomb in the 17th century
  • Another player chose the path of immortality, successfully attained enlightenment through Daoist cultivation, and struck Huang Taiji down with a single lightning spell
  • Yet another player formed a line infantry regiment, replacing cold-weapon armies with muskets and bayonets

In this game, the only limit is your imagination.

Pricing Model: A Real-World Topic

The game uses a ¥48 buy-to-play + Token microtransaction hybrid model.

  • The base purchase includes an initial token bundle, supporting approximately 30 hours of gameplay in fast mode
  • Once tokens run out, purchase more as needed: ¥1 = 100 tokens
  • Fast mode consumes roughly 100-200 tokens per hour, i.e., ¥1-2 per hour
  • Expert mode consumes tokens approximately 4 times faster than fast mode

This model has generated considerable controversy on Steam, with the game’s positive review rating once hovering around 54%. Developer Zhuiqing’s explanation is candid: every simulation run incurs real AI inference costs. The team requires output speeds of 80-120 tokens/second (the industry standard is 50-60/second) to ensure players wait no more than 2 minutes, plus server-side costs supporting thousands of concurrent players — tokens are currently sold essentially at cost.

Objectively speaking, this is not a traditional buy-to-play payment experience. But from another perspective, every yuan you spend is real computing power weaving this historical world for you. The moment you issue a command, a model with tens of billions of parameters is simulating an alternate timeline based on your decision — this was still science fiction five years ago.

Is It Worth Buying?

👍 It Will Surprise You If:

  • You’re tired of traditional historical games that offer “the illusion of freedom but only two real paths”
  • You want to experience true narrative freedom — write the royal edicts you want, and watch the AI take every decision seriously
  • You’re curious about how AI technology is changing the gaming industry
  • You enjoy watching your decisions trigger unexpected chain reactions (both good and bad)

👎 You Might Want to Think Again If:

  • You have a natural aversion to token-based microtransactions
  • You expect polished graphics and cutscenes — this game is indeed quite sparse in traditional narrative presentation
  • You can’t tolerate the occasional “OOC” (out-of-character behavior) or memory inconsistencies from the AI
  • You’re looking for a fully polished traditional commercial game rather than an experimental work

Notable Statistics

  • Day-1 retention rate: 75.1% (2-3 times the industry average)
  • Average daily playtime on non-workdays: approximately 7 hours
  • Steam reviews: over 1,200 and counting
  • Peak concurrent players: over 1,000
  • Official simulation accuracy rate: 98.7%

These numbers reflect an interesting phenomenon — players complain endlessly, yet can’t stop playing. This is perhaps the fate of being “the first to eat crab”: it’s not perfect, but it shows you what’s possible.

Future Plans

The development team has publicly shared their roadmap:

  • Custom API Interface — allowing players to bring their own models or API keys, fundamentally solving the token cost issue
  • Workshop Support — enabling players to create custom scenarios and events
  • Ongoing optimization of AI simulation stability and character consistency

Closing Thoughts

“Historical Simulator: Chongzhen” is not a perfect game, but it is a milestone.

It is the first commercially released AI-native game that uses a large language model as its core gameplay mechanism rather than a peripheral feature. Its successes and failures provide invaluable data and experience for those who follow in this space.

If you’re interested in the direction of “AI games,” if you want to experience what it feels like to “change the world with natural language,” spending ¥48 to be emperor for a while is likely one of the best value experiences in 2026.
