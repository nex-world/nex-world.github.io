AI-Native Games Panorama Report (2025–2026)

From Steam indie darlings to major-studio systems with tens of millions of DAU, from a text adventure made by a solo developer in a single week to a party game with 20 million players — AI is fundamentally redefining what a “game” can be.

Preface: What Exactly Are “AI-Native Games”?

Before we dive in, a definition is in order.

The “AI games” we see today fall into three tiers:

TierDescriptionExamples
AI-Assisted ProductionAI tools used for art, voice acting, or design, but the game remains traditionally designedMost AAA titles today
AI-Enhanced FeaturesAI as a subsystem — AI teammates, AI NPC dialoguePeacekeeper Elite AI Teammates, AI NPC Mods
AI-Native GamesLLM/generative AI is the core gameplay driver — without AI, the game ceases to existChongzhen Simulator, AI Roguelite, Death by AI 

This report focuses on the third tier — AI-native games. Their defining characteristic: you are no longer playing through pre-scripted content; instead, you’re interacting in real time with a creative AI system.

Chapter 1: Steam Indie Games — The Vanguard of AI-Native Gaming

Independent developers were the earliest explorers of AI-native games. Unburdened by AAA studio overhead, they were the first to put LLMs at the heart of gameplay.

1.1 AI Roguelite

ItemInfo
DeveloperMax Loh (solo indie developer)
Steam Price$14.99 USD
Rating83% (Very Positive)
Core ConceptThe world’s first RPG where “every entity is AI-generated”
Steam Linkhttps://store.steampowered.com/app/1889620/AI_Roguelite/ 

In a nutshell: Your Dungeon Master is an omnipotent, always-online AI.

The game has no pre-built maps, monsters, equipment, or crafting recipes — everything is generated in real time by a large language model. It truly realizes the concept of an “infinite RPG”: every new save file opens a world that has never existed before. The combat system operates on a three-layer logic of “plausibility check + dice roll + AI narration,” making absurd scenarios like “defeating a Terminator with raw salmon” possible.

Why it matters: It is the most complete fusion of “AI freedom” and “traditional RPG structure” on the market today, proving that an LLM can serve as a full game engine rather than a mere辅助 tool. Since its 2023 launch, it has received continuous updates, and a new DLC was released in June 2026.

1.2 History Simulator: Chongzhen (崇祯模拟器)

ItemInfo
DeveloperQingGan Studio (< 10 people)
Steam Price¥48 (buy-to-play) + Token microtransactions
Rating~54% (Mixed)
Release DateMay 8, 2026
Steam Linkhttps://store.steampowered.com/app/4304230/_/ 

In a nutshell: Write imperial edicts in natural language to govern a dynasty, as the AI simulates the butterfly effects of the Ming Empire.

You play as the Chongzhen Emperor in 1627. Every crisis you face has no preset options — you write your edicts directly, and the AI’s three-layer simulation system (state constraints → intent evaluation → state persistence) determines how your governance decisions trigger chain reactions.

Why it matters: It is China’s first commercially released AI-native game, pioneering the controversial “buy-to-play + Token” model. With a Day-2 retention rate of 75% and an average session length of 7 hours, the data proves that players are indeed willing to pay for AI-driven gameplay experiences, even though the pricing model still needs refinement.

1.3 Microverse In Box (盒中小世界)

ItemInfo
DeveloperKsanaDock (solo indie developer)
LicenseMIT (free and open source)
Steam StatusComing soon, wishlist now open
Steam Linkhttps://store.steampowered.com/app/3902630/Microverse_In_Box/ 

In a nutshell: Raise a society of 8 AI characters inside your own computer.

Eight AI characters each possess independent personalities, long-term memory, and autonomous agency. They work, socialize, fall in love, and argue in a small town — while you observe and intervene from a god’s-eye view. Built on the Godot 4 engine, all logic runs within the engine itself with no need for a separate Python backend.

Why it matters: It serves as the bridge that brings the “Stanford AI Town” from academic experiment to consumer product. With an MIT open-source license, multi-platform support, compatibility with 7 AI models, and Ollama local deployment, it lowers the barrier to experiencing AI society simulation to nearly zero — download, open, plug in an API key, and play.

1.4 Legend of Zhaoyang (昭阳传)

ItemInfo
DeveloperXVERSE
Steam Rating89%
PlatformSteam
Core MechanicAI agent-driven dynamic narrative Roguelike
Steam LinkSearch “昭阳传” on Steam

In a nutshell: NPCs hold grudges, repay favors, and engage in factional politics — even after your kingdom falls, the story continues.

Legend of Zhaoyang is one of the earliest AI-native games in China and among the highest-rated AI games on Steam. Every NPC is driven by an AI agent with an independent personality and memory system. Today’s decisions reshape tomorrow’s relationship web, and even after the “fall of the kingdom,” rich side branches of hermitage, revenge, and rebuilding await.

Why it matters: An 89% positive rating is among the highest for AI-native games in China, proving that AI-driven NPC interaction can deliver immersion that surpasses traditional scripted storytelling.

1.5 Whispers from the Star (星之低语)

ItemInfo
DeveloperAnuttacon (Cai Haoyu, former miHoYo founder)
Steam Price$9.99 USD (¥33.99)
Rating93-95% on launch day
Release DateAugust 15, 2025
Steam Linkhttps://store.steampowered.com/app/3730100/Whispers_from_the_Star/ 

In a nutshell: You are the only person on Earth who can reach a girl stranded on an alien planet. You communicate with her via voice and video — her expressions, tone, and emotions are all generated by AI in real time.

This is Cai Haoyu (the “father of Genshin Impact”)‘s venture after leaving miHoYo. The game defines a new genre — FPT (First-Person Talker). There are no pre-scripted dialogue trees; all communication is open-ended, real-time AI conversation. Stella’s facial expressions and body language are generated in real time by AI combined with motion capture data, and she possesses long-term memory.

Why it matters: This is the “father of Genshin Impact“‘s ultimate bet on AI gaming — Anuttacon’s team of roughly 50 people includes nearly half from miHoYo. Although Day-1 concurrent players were only ~964, the Steam rating sits at 93-95%. It represents the frontier of AI games in the direction of “multimodal real-time interaction.”

ItemInfo
DeveloperTBD

Steam Linkhttps://store.steampowered.com/app/4596790/Hostage_Down/ 

In a nutshell: A negotiation simulation where you talk directly to a hostage-taker — type or speak, and the AI responds in real time.

No pre-scripted dialogue trees. Your tone, strategy, and approach are all parsed and responded to by the LLM in real time. Every negotiation is unique.

Why it matters: It represents a new direction for AI games — high-pressure, high-immersion scenario simulations. This genre was nearly impossible to implement within traditional frameworks (branching costs were too high), but in the LLM era it has become a naturally fitting category.

Chapter 2: Major Studios Enter the Fray — AI as a “Super-System”

Unlike indie developers, major studios’ AI game strategy is to integrate AI as an enhancement to existing products rather than building new games from scratch. The advantage: enormous existing user bases.

2.1 Justice (逆水寒)  Mobile · Dreamweaver Mode (NetEase)

Launched on April 3, 2026. Players direct the story’s progression through text or voice commands, with the AI generating scenes, characters, dialogue, and endings in real time. It topped the mobile game popularity charts upon release.

AI-Driven Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (AI is the core of gameplay)

2.2 Justice (逆水寒)  Mobile · AI Retainer System (NetEase)

Players can customize their AI retainers’ appearance, personality, voice, and backstory. AI retainers have long-term memory, and their emotions evolve over time. The most touching case: a player used it to recreate departed loved ones and “converse” with them again in the game.

AI-Driven Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (AI is the core of emotional connection)

2.3 Peacekeeper Elite (和平精英) · AI Teammate System (Tencent)

Link: Peacekeeper Elite Official Site 

“Absolute Commander 2.0.” Covers 110 million players with 17.7 million daily active users. AI teammates understand tactical jargon, autonomously provide cover and revive teammates, and engage in voice chat banter. It may be the largest AI game system in the world by user count.

AI-Driven Level: ⭐⭐⭐ (AI is a subsystem, but the experience difference is massive)

2.4 Paranormal Task Force (超自然行动组) · AI Challenge Mode (Giant Network)

Fully launched in January 2026. AI-driven “bots” imitate player behavior and speech patterns, going undercover and launching sneak attacks — making it impossible to tell if you’re up against a human or AI. Within two months of launch, AI matches surpassed 25 million.

AI-Driven Level: ⭐⭐⭐⭐ (AI transforms the core PvP experience)

2.5 Eggy Party (蛋仔派对) · Model AIGC (NetEase)

Players describe what they want in natural language to generate levels and skins. Zero-barrier UGC creation, with cumulative player-created content exceeding one million items.

AI-Driven Level: ⭐⭐⭐ (AIGC tool, not core gameplay)

Chapter 3: Party & Social — The Biggest Dark Horse of AI-Native Gaming

3.1 Death by AI

ItemInfo
DeveloperLittle Umbrella (Playroom platform)
Players20 million in 3 months
Peak DAU700,000 DAU (within 3 days of launch)
PlatformsDiscord, Web, iOS
PriceFree
Funding$2M led by a16z Speedrun

Link: https://deathbyai.gg  (free, playable in browser)

In a nutshell: You write a “survival plan,” and the AI judges whether you live or die — like a Jackbox party game with an AI referee.

Eight players take turns writing survival strategies (e.g., “What do you do if you fall into a shark tank?”). The AI judge evaluates the logic and creativity of each plan to decide who lives and who dies. The game mechanics are feather-light, but the social virality is immense — everyone understands the rules, and everyone wants to watch their friends “die.”

Why it matters: It is currently the most commercially successful AI-native game. 20 million players, 700,000 DAU, profitable within 3 months — the data proves that AI gaming’s “killer app” may not live on Steam but on social platforms.

4.3 Trends to Watch

  1. The Token Economics Game: Chongzhen Simulator’s “buy-to-play + Token” model has sparked intense debate, but it’s an unavoidable question for AI game monetization. The winner will be whoever finds the pricing sweet spot players can accept.

  2. Vibe Coding Destroys the Development Barrier: The January 2026 “AI Text Adventure” wave was just the beginning. When the technical barrier to game development approaches zero, creativity and operations become the only competitive moats.

  3. Social Platforms > Steam Store: Death by AI exploded on Discord; AI text adventures spread via WeChat and Xiaohongshu. The cold-start path for AI-native games may be fundamentally different from traditional games.

  4. AI NPCs Become Standard Equipment: From Justice (逆水寒)  to Peacekeeper Elite (和平精英) , major studios are embedding AI NPCs into existing products. By 2027, “NPCs that can’t hold a natural-language conversation” may feel as dated as “NPCs without voice acting.”

  5. Open-Source Ecosystem Accelerates: KsanaDock’s MIT license and the spread of Ollama local deployment — the underlying technology of AI games is rapidly democratizing.

Conclusion: Is This the iPhone Moment for AI Games?

Looking back to 2007 when the iPhone first launched, most people thought it was just “an iPod with a touchscreen.” It wasn’t until the App Store launched and developers flooded in that the industry realized — a new era had begun.

AI-native games in 2025–2026 may be at a similar inflection point.

  • Death by AI  proved that AI games can go viral and achieve commercial sustainability
  • Chongzhen Simulator proved that traditional buy-to-play users are willing to pay for AI inference
  • AI Roguelite proved that an LLM can serve as a complete game engine
  • Fan-made “simulator” titles proved that solo developers can create hits in a week using AI
  • Justice (逆水寒)  and Peacekeeper Elite (和平精英)  proved that major studios have fully entered the arena

The question is no longer “Will AI change games?” but “How much time do traditional games have left?”

🌐

From NEXWORLD: We believe AI will fundamentally transform the way games tell stories. Every game mentioned in this report is, in its own way, exploring the answer to the same question: When AI can give games infinite possibilities, do we still need “pre-designed content”?
There is no definitive answer yet — but the exploration itself is the most exciting thing about this era.
